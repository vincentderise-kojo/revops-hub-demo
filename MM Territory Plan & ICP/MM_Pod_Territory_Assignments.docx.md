# **Overview & Design Principles**

Each Senior AE owns two pod territories supported by a dedicated SDR. Territories are anchored by 2+ marquee Kojo ENT accounts within the market — these become the name-drop social proof that drives credibility in outreach. The SDR runs concentrated outbound within those geographic clusters; the AE directs the motion and handles executive engagement.

* Territory ownership belongs to the AE permanently — SDR assignments are operational and can change

**SDR Assignment Rules**

The following rules govern how SDR assignments work, what AEs can count on, and what happens when SDRs move or leave. These exist so AEs never feel like their territory is held hostage by SDR turnover.

**Core Principle**

The territory and its named account book belong to the AE. The SDR is operational support layered on top for purposes of prospecting. An SDR leaving, getting promoted, or being reassigned never changes the AE’s territory definition, account ownership, or book size.

**If an SDR Gets Promoted to ENT**

* The AE’s territory does not change. The account book stays intact.

* The AE gets public recognition for developing that SDR into an ENT-ready rep — called out explicitly by Jeremy in a team setting, not just a Slack emoji

  * Bonus if their SDR gets promoted ($2k?)

* The AE gets first input on backfill. Before Sadie assigns a replacement, Jeremy asks: “What did you need most from your last SDR? What should we look for in the next one?” That input is factored into the assignment. The AE does not have final say — that belongs to SDR management — but their input is weighted.

* Backfill does not have to be a new hire. In periods where Kojo is carrying a 6th MM SDR — typically done proactively to get ahead of anticipated attrition or a likely ENT promotion — that SDR may be assigned to the vacated pair directly. When no 6th SDR is available, Sadie will assign the best available SDR from within the pool, which may include rebalancing an existing pair if it serves overall system health. The goal is the best pairing, not the simplest rotation.

**If an SDR Leaves or Is Let Go**

* The AE’s territory and account book do not change. No other AE benefits from the vacancy.

* When Kojo is carrying a 6th MM SDR, that SDR provides dedicated coverage to the affected AE while backfill is finalized. When no 6th SDR exists — which is the normal state — Sadie will assess whether partial coverage can be redistributed from within the existing pool without materially disrupting other pairs. The AE should expect some reduction in SDR output during the transition window and plan self-sourced outbound accordingly.

* Leadership commits to a 30-day backfill window. If backfill is not complete in 30 days, the AE receives a timeline update and additional support options from Jeremy.

* Same backfill input rights apply as in the promotion scenario — the AE provides input before Sadie finalizes the new assignment.

**SDR Reassignment Between Pairs**

* AEs cannot request a different SDR. SDR managers cannot unilaterally reassign. Any pair change is a joint decision by Jeremy and Sadie, with a documented rationale.

* SDR assignments are made based on what’s best for the overall pod system — not AE seniority or preference. An SDR who has built 6 months of territory context in a pairing will not be pulled simply because another AE wants them.

**AE 1s — No Dedicated SDR**

* AE 1s do not receive a dedicated SDR. They work inbound and self-source. This is intentional — earning a dedicated SDR is a function of reaching Senior AE level, creating a clear incentive for development.

**The AE Guarantee**

These are the commitments Kojo makes to every Senior AE in this territory model:

* Your territory and account book are yours. An SDR leaving never changes your book.

* If a 6th MM SDR is active at the time of a vacancy, you will have dedicated coverage within days. If not — which is the normal state — Sadie will work to redistribute partial SDR support as quickly as possible. You will always be informed of the timeline and what to expect.

* You will be consulted before your backfill SDR is assigned. Your input on what you need in that role is taken seriously.

* You will receive full credit and public recognition when your SDR earns an ENT promotion. Developing your SDR is a performance positive, not a loss.

* Territories are reviewed on a fixed quarterly schedule. No surprise mid-quarter reassignments.

* Each AE also retains their existing hunting ground (50-80 accounts) worked independently via self-sourced outbound

* Larger MM accounts outside the marquee cluster are Tier 2 within each territory — AE owns outbound on these directly. This includes our largest MM accounts nationally regardless of geography: any MM account at the top of the revenue range is too valuable to sit unworked in a round-robin pool. These are explicitly named in each AE’s book and the AE is accountable for activity on them

* Inbound accounts outside named territories are distributed round-robin

* Remarcable is the only relevant competitor — they focus exclusively on electrical. Midwest and PNW markets carry higher risk; mechanical-first markets are largely uncontested

| *The name-drop thesis: SDRs run concentrated outreach within a cluster using marquee ENT accounts as instant credibility. 'We work with \[Big State Electric\] and \[Alterman\] in your market' resonates because local contractors know those names. This only works if SDRs stay in the cluster and don't context-switch constantly.* |

# **Pod Operating Rules**

## **Account Tiers**

| Tier | Definition |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Tier 1** | Geo-clustered accounts within marquee ENT anchor radius. SDR runs concentrated name-drop outbound here. Primary pod motion. |
| **Tier 2** | Larger MM accounts (top 20% by revenue) outside the geo cluster but within the territory (may not be geo-based). Includes our largest MM accounts nationally — any account at the high end of the MM revenue range is named explicitly in an AE’s book regardless of geography, and the AE is accountable for outbound activity. AE owns all Tier 2 outreach directly — not SDR-supported. |
| **Hunting Ground** | AE's existing 50-80 accounts. AE self-sources independently. No SDR involvement. Tracked separately in Salesforce. |
| **RR Inbound** | Accounts outside all named territories that come inbound. Distributed round-robin. No AE can claim based on proximity alone. |

## **Account Request Process**

If an AE or SDR identifies a strong-fit account outside their territory, they can request it be added to their named book. Process:

* Submit to Jeremy and Sadie with a one-line rationale

* Decision within 48 hours

* Maximum one request per week per pair to prevent distraction

* Approved accounts are added to the territory permanently — they don't revert

## **Territory Review Cadence**

* Territories are reviewed quarterly only — no mid-quarter rebalancing

* Changes only occur if: a new AE joins, an AE departs, or significant account density imbalance is demonstrated with data

* SDR assignments can change operationally at any time — territory ownership does not change with them

## **Remarcable Displacement Rules**

| Market Type | Outbound Approach |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Texas, Florida, Southeast** | Full MEP motion — electrical and mechanical both in play. Lead with electrical name drops where anchors exist. |
| **DC/Baltimore, NYC/NJ** | Mixed — test electrical with sharper competitive messaging. Mechanical accounts are lower risk and should be prioritized. |
| **Atlanta, Nashville, Phoenix** | Mechanical-first but electrical not off limits. Use judgment on account by account basis. |
| **Minneapolis, Midwest** | Mechanical ONLY in outbound sequencing. Do not target electrical accounts — Remarcable is entrenched. Mechanical is fully uncontested. |
| **PNW (hunting ground only)** | Mechanical-first in self-sourced outbound. Electrical is Remarcable territory. |
